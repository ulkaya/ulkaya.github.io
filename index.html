What Is Bioluminescence?

Bioluminescence is the natural ability of living organisms to produce light through internal chemical reactions involving luciferin and luciferase.

This form of light production is extremely efficient and produces almost no heat, which is why it is referred to as cold light.

The image above shows a naturally glowing organism, where light is used as a tool for communication and survival.

Key Facts

Cold Light

Bioluminescent organisms convert chemical energy directly into light, wasting very little energy as heat.

Ocean Dominance

A majority of deep-sea organisms are capable of producing light, as sunlight does not reach those depths.

Scientific Use

Bioluminescent proteins are widely used in medical and biological research to track cells and study diseases.
